About Skepticamp NYC

Where Curiosity
Gets a Microphone

Skepticamp NYC is a free, one-day unconference where anyone can present a 15 or 30-minute talk on topics touching science, skepticism, or critical thinking. No credentials required. Just bring your curiosity and an idea worth sharing.

Skepticamp NYC has been gathering curious New Yorkers since 2009. What started as a small gathering of skeptics, scientists, and science enthusiasts has grown into one of New York City's most beloved annual intellectual events.

The unconference format means there's no hierarchy of speakers. The person who gives the opening talk might be a PhD researcher — or a passionate hobbyist who spent six months debunking a popular myth. Both are equally welcome. Both are equally valued.

We're organized by the New York City Skeptics, a non-profit dedicated to promoting science literacy and critical thinking in the five boroughs and beyond. The event is free — always has been, always will be. We accept optional donations to cover venue costs and keep the lights on.

Past talks have covered everything from vaccine science to astrology debunking, from the psychology of conspiracy theories to the history of the FDA. If it involves evidence-based reasoning, you'll find it here.

What We Stand For

Our Values

Openness

All perspectives are welcome as long as they engage honestly with evidence. We don't require credentials — we require intellectual honesty.

Community

We're not a lecture hall — we're a gathering of peers. Every attendee is also a potential presenter. The best conversations happen in the hallways.

Evidence

We celebrate skepticism — not cynicism. We follow the evidence wherever it leads, even when it challenges our priors. Especially then.

Safe Space for Ideas

Code of Conduct

All participants are expected to engage respectfully. We do not tolerate harassment or discrimination of any kind. Disagreement is healthy; disrespect is not. By registering, you agree to uphold these standards.
